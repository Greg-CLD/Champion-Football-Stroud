# Homepage Design Intent (Champion Football Stroud)

## Quick summary
This homepage is designed for young children first, and parents second. It should feel like a colourful football playground: bold, playful, easy to scan, and full of friendly energy.

The homepage now uses one clear learning model:

- `Skills`
  - `Essentials`
  - `Practice Areas`
- `Videos`
  - `Football Highlights`
  - `Cool Moments`

Future edits should extend this playful direction rather than turning the page into a generic adult website.

## Audience and experience intent

### Primary audience: young children
- The page should feel exciting and easy to understand in a few seconds.
- Visuals should be bold, obvious, and rewarding.
- Cards, chunky buttons, and short labels should do most of the work.

### Secondary audience: parents
- Parents should still be able to scan the page quickly.
- Parent-facing content should stay clear, calm, and trustworthy while fitting the same child-first visual language.

## Information architecture to preserve

### 1. One homepage Skills section
- Do not bring back separate homepage sections for:
  - Key Football Skills
  - Skills learned
  - Skills practised
- Keep one Skills section only.

### 2. Two mental models inside Skills
- `Essentials` = the core football actions children practise.
- `Practice Areas` = simple match jobs with short coaching rules.

### 3. Videos split
- The main Videos area should only contain:
  - Football Highlights
  - Cool Moments
- Skills training videos belong inside the Skills area, not the main Videos area.

## Visual style principles to preserve

### 1. Colour system: bright and saturated
- Keep strong football-friendly colours such as sky blue, grass green, sunny yellow, orange, and deep blue.
- Use colour to separate sections and help children understand where they are.

### 2. Shape language: chunky, rounded, tactile
- Keep rounded corners, thick borders, and visible depth.
- Buttons and cards should still feel pressable and playful.

### 3. Typography: bold and playful
- Preserve loud, celebratory headings.
- Keep body copy short and easy to read.

### 4. Card-based browsing
- Skills and Practice Areas should feel like collectible cards or game tiles.
- Detail should open inside the same homepage section rather than sending children deep into subpages.

## Interaction style
- Keep interactions simple, obvious, and immediate.
- Use two clear tabs in Skills:
  - Essentials
  - Practice Areas
- Use two clear tabs in Videos:
  - Football Highlights
  - Cool Moments
- Avoid deep navigation or busy nested UI.

## Content and copy tone
- Use short, upbeat, child-friendly sentences.
- Prefer football words children know: pass, move, shoot, defend, space, team.
- Keep explanations short and positive.

## Must-preserve features
- Bright football colour palette and strong contrast.
- Chunky rounded cards and buttons with visible depth.
- One clear Skills section with the two mental models.
- Child-friendly summaries and shallow navigation.
- Forest Green highlights loaded from the existing JSON feed.

## What to avoid
- Bringing back the old split between learned and practised sections.
- Putting skills training back into the main Videos area.
- Adding lots of deep subpages.
- Muted, corporate, or minimalist redesign choices that remove the page personality.

## Working rule
Before finalizing any homepage change, check this:

Does the page still feel like a fun, colourful football playground for young children, while keeping the new Skills and Videos structure clear?

If not, revise it until it does.
